Summary

Cisco Learning Scinece team is trying to bring more realistic problem-based learning experience that can complement traditional 2D screen-based learning. My team envisions the next-generation learning experience of Cisco Networking Academy, a global IT-networking education program.

I'm the first user experience researcher in Cisco Learning Science. As a UXR, I'm leading UX discussions by conducting the various types of design researches along the product development cycle.


Project 1. Problem-based Learning in 3D VR Space

Cisco is trying to create a 3D immersive problem-based learning experience in VR based on real-life settings. My team believes hands-on learning is critical to teach and learn hands-on skills, especially for entry-level engineers with less experience in industry settings. In Cisco NetAcad VR, users encounter real-life networking problems in a data center that they need to solve using their hands and they troubleshoot the identified problems as IT-network enginnering professionals.

I'm a lead UXR for Cisco NetAcad VR and Cisco VR-based certification exam. I set up a research framework for the team and lead iterative design research on 3D immersive learning experience for online training and summative assessment.

Project 2. Problem-based learning on 2D Simulation Tool

After COVID-19, Cisco Networking Academy invests more in improving online distance learning experience. Rapid prototyping group is actively working on improving Packet Tracer, a 2D visual simulation software for IT-networking, so instructors and learners can get more practical 2D simulating experience based on real-life scenarios.

My team has a small rapid prototyping group to experiment various product ideas of distance learning. For radid prototyping, I'm responsible for taking care of user experience and creating design mockups.

Prototyping work mostly runs on the 2-week sprint to turn an early product idea to a minimum viable product. Once we get the experiment outcome at the end of the sprint, we set a larger group meeting with various stakeholders to demonstrate our idea and make a product decision. Once the team decides to continue the work to productize the idea, my team expands the prototyping work and starts working with production team to make it happen.


Methods

  • Qualitative: Heuristic evaluation, Ethnographic study, Focus group, Moderated/Unmoderated usability testing
  • Quantitative: Survey at scale
