Summary

Around The Corner was born in 2024 from my hobby project started one year before.

After pandemic, I had a hobby project meeting two strangers every week over coffee. I found strangers through my anonymous writing on social media. Soon after 3 months, my project went viral to have 150+ people on the waitlist. I thought this should be a platform where people can discover each other beyond 1:1 with me, and introduced the early concept of the community using Google Form and Sheet. In the same year, I invited one of the strangers to be my technical co-founder and we launched our first website in January 2024.

Fast forward to September 2025, Around The Corner grew to serve 2500+ users across California organically and achieved 38% 3-month user retention. We partnered with 5 hospitality and F&B businesses to test B2B opportunities and hosted 30+ group events. Majority of our events were sold out with 93+ participation rate and extremly low no-shows. Although we successfully validated the product-user fit, we faced the limitation of the platform design to achieve the product-market fit and extend our runway. In September 2025, we decided to wind down the platform and rebuild Around The Corner from scratch.

What we build

Around The Corner users used their lived experience to introduce themselves and turn them into new connections that lead to IRL coffee chats in town.

Around The Corner accepted users through our application process where they answer to five long-form writing prompts we provided. The five prompts were carefully crafted to allow users introduce themselves based on their lived experiences, not using self-staged photos or superficial information such as age, sex, job, or ethnicity. Our platform only allowed one faceless photo describing their everyday life or their recent speical moments.

Around The Corner allowed users to add more depth to their profile through journaling. Based on those approved users' long-form writings, we used AI to generate follow-up questions to help users deepen their stories. GPT-3.5 and Claude Sonnet 4 were used for AI-guided journaling experience.

Around The Corner also hosted monthly gatherings to offer community experiences in real life. We partnered with 5 local hospitality and F&B businesses in SF Bay Area, Greater Los Angeles, and Orange County area to explore B2B partnership opportunities.
