Smart Birdhouse

A field study of STEAM education development

ID Lab in Ewha Womans University developed STEAM education curriculum and conducted its 6-week pilot program with 2 teachers and 30 students at Imae Elementary School. STEAM is an educational approach to learning that uses Science, Technology, Engineering, the Arts and Mathematics as access points for guiding student inquiry, dialogue, and critical thinking. ID Lab suggested an 4-subject integrated education program including outdoor activities based on 3rd-4th grade Science I, Science II, Mathematics, and Art curriculums.

The smart birdhouse is made with corrugated cardboard with various craft materials. It contains a light sensor and a flexible numeric display screen inside to let the students monitor the birds’ visits. When the birds enter through the round hole on the front side, the light sensor(in-put) is overshadowed and flexible numeric display screen(out-put) prints the number of the birds’ visits. Two-week field trial with 2 teachers and 34 students was conducted to evaluate the education program.

The curriculum includes 6 sessions. In the session 1 and 2, students learn about the birds habitat in the urban environment, and how the electronic appliances are working with in-put and out-put devices. Students can understand how the smart birdhouse works with a light sensor and a flexible numeric display.
